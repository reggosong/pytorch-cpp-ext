1 · The math

SmoothReLU replaces ReLU's kink at zero with a quadratic ramp of width α. The pieces are chosen so both f and f′ are continuous — at x = α the quadratic and linear pieces meet at exactly α/2, with matching slope 1.

f(x) f′(x) x ≤ 0 → 0 ramp x²/2α linear x−α/2

3 · Benchmarks (real measurements)

vs PyTorch's native fused activations (forward, n = 10⁸)
